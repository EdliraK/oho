Introduction back to ToC

This ontology aims to provide the DfMA communitywith a vocabulary of commonly understood DfMA conceptsand relationships to represent the domain of offsite construction. Furthermore, this ontology supports the publication and use of semantically well-defined data across the DfMA production chain (linked DfMA data). This is achieved by contributing to the development of a domain knowledge ontology thathandles interdisciplinary information exchange among different participants during the life-cycle of design for manufacturing. OHO provides a high-level description of assemblies,sub-assemblies, products, the interface used for connecting andmore deeply the production method used and resources neededfor the production of parts and assemblies used in DfMA pro-cesses.
